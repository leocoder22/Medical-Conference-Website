About 3rd MAHATROPACON 2026

The Indian Academy of Tropical Parasitology (IATP), established in 2007 under the leadership of S. C. Parija, has significantly advanced tropical parasitology in India by promoting research, education, and interdisciplinary collaboration across protozoology and helminthology.

MAHATROPACON 2026, the 3rd Maharashtra State Chapter Conference, to be hosted at Datta Meghe Medical College, represents an important step in strengthening networks among microbiologists, parasitologists, clinicians, and researchers addressing parasitic diseases of public health relevance. The theme, “Breaking Silos – Innovative Solutions for Parasitic Diseases,” emphasizes the need for integrated, multidisciplinary approaches linking diagnostics, clinical care, research, and public health.

The conference will feature expert lectures, panel discussions, and interactive sessions, including pre-conference workshops on advanced diagnostic methodologies. It aims to provide a robust platform for knowledge exchange, academic collaboration, and the development of innovative strategies to address parasitic diseases.

About Nagpur

Nagpur, known as the Orange City of India and the geographical centre of the country, is strategically located with excellent connectivity by air, rail, and road, making it a key administrative, educational, and commercial hub of central India. The city holds historical significance with Deekshabhoomi, associated with B. R. Ambedkar, while landmarks such as Zero Mile Stone and Futala Lake add cultural and recreational value.

Nagpur also serves as a gateway to prominent wildlife reserves, including Tadoba–Andhari Tiger Reserve (~140 km) and Pench Tiger Reserve (~90 km), both renowned for rich biodiversity and tiger sightings.

The city’s culinary identity is highlighted by its bold Saoji cuisine, along with local specialties such as tarri poha and patodi rassa. With its blend of accessibility, cultural heritage, and academic vibrancy, Nagpur provides an ideal setting for MAHATROPACON 2026.
